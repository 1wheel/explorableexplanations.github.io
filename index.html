EXPLORABLE EXPLANATIONS

“I hear and I forget. I see and I remember. I do and I understand.”
~ Confucius

“Explorable Explanations” was coined by Bret Victor in a stunning essay that mixed interactivity with text & pictures. His article isn't just a passive monologue—it engages the reader in active dialogue.

Explorable Explanations by Bret Victor


This seed has slowly sprouted a movement. People made programming tutorials, embedded with examples of the very thing that they wanted to teach you to create.

Red Blob Games by Amit Patel
Sight & Light by Nicky Case


Then, people made explorable explanations of other computational and mathematical models. Concepts and algorithms usually frighteningly hard to understand became intuitive playthings!

Explained Visually by Victor Powell & Lewis Lehe
Visualizing Algorithms by Mike Bostock


But why stop at computational/mathematical models? Folks expanded this form of active learning to explain complicated systems from the natural sciences and social sciences.

Earth: A Primer by Chaim Gingold
Parable of the Polygons by Vi Hart & Nicky Case


Even the chaos of the daily world could be made playable. A few journalists transformed data into models, and let their readers explore real economic and political systems.

Turning A Corner? by Amanda Cox
Who Will Win The Senate? by Mike Bostock, Shan Carter, Amanda Cox, Jennifer Daniel, Josh Katz & Kevin Quealy


In all these wide and varied examples, the reader is active. The reader asks questions, and finds answers. The reader proves lessons to themselves. The reader gains an intuitive understanding of complex systems. The reader creates hypothetical worlds. The reader has a conversation with the author.

This is a powerful new tool for thinking... and we've only just begun.

We hope you'll join us in exploring Explorable Explanations.
